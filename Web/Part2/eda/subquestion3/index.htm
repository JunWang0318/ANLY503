Sub-Question 3: Is There a Relationship between Cancer Incidence Rate and Income, Smoking, Diabetes, and Obesity?


We first created an interactive correlation matrix and showed it on a heatmap below. The viewer is able to select the features that he or she is interested in then the corresponding correlation matrix, (Interactive Shiny App Figure 9) will be generated. From a comprehensive comparison, we selected all the features and could see that the top two factors related to cancer incidence rate are smoking and diabetes.


Then we decided to conduct hypothesis tests on the relationship between smoking and cancer rate as well as between diabetes and cancer incidence rate by linear regression. As a result from part 1, even though all parameters are statistically significant, the R-squared for both regression lines are really small. Therefore, we are not able to conclude that there is some relationship between smoking and cancer rate, or between diabetes and cancer incidence rate.


In addition to the previous linear regression, we decided to plot the multiple regression for the cancer rate by using plotly 3D scatter plot.



Figure 10 (Plotly Rotatable 3D)


Figure 11 (R)

The result was consistent with the previous finding. All the parameters were statistically significant but with really small the R-squared. Ultimately, we were not able to conclude that there is some relationship between smoking or diabetes to cancer incidence rate.
