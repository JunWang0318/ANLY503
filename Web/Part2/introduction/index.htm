Introduction


Figure 1 (Tableau Wordcloud)

What are the common causes of death in the United States? According to the data from world health organization [1], various types of cancers and heart disease are the world’s biggest killers and these diseases have remained the leading causes of death globally for more than a decade. Since cancers are one of the biggest causes of death, we decided to make further investigation and analysis about it.



Figure 2 (Matplotlib)


Here is the graph for trending of cancer incidence rate and mortality rate from 1999 to 2015. Both rates represent the number of count per 100,000 people. In general, the mortality rate for cancer is decreasing or about the same since 1999 while the values for incidence rate keep increasing. Therefore, it will be interesting and meaningful for us to identify the potential factors that cause or related to cancers.


In the United States, cancer incidence and mortality rates differ a lot between different states.



Figure 3 (Python word cloud)


The word cloud above shows cancer incidence rate for each state in the United States. Bigger font represents higher cancer incidence rate for that state. From the graph, it seems like that Louisiana, Kentucky, Mississippi and Ohio have relatively higher cancer incidence rate compared to other states. It would be interesting to investigate reasons why that some states have higher cancer rate than others.


Overall, we want to investigate the relationship between the aggregated cancer rates and the geographic location, economic and agricultural factors, people’s health condition as well as median household income across the United States, from 1999 to 2015. As mentioned at the beginning, it is meaningful to study these relationships because cancers are the leading cause of death for more than a decade and people are all concerned about cancers prevention and put great effort into reducing the overall cancers rates. In our study, we will take a look at this relationship by various main factors year to year. As a result, based on different types of visualizations we provided, people will have a better overall understanding about the trending of the aggregated cancer rates as well as its related features. We collected cancer data, health condition, and income data as well as economic and agricultural factors data. More information about data collection and data cleaning can be found here.
