Sub-Question 2: What are the top five cancer types in the United States? What characteristics do they have?



Figure 7 (Tableau)


From the above area charts, we can see that the number of incidences for the top five cancers are about the same, however, the mortality number for lung cancer was significantly higher than all others since 1999. So we focused on the death rate for lung cancer across the United States and created a Tableau map showing the death rate of lung cancer for each state.


Figure 8 (Tableau)


The viewers are able to adjust the time period they are interested in by using the Year filter on the upper right corner. The percentage shown on the map is the average death rate of lung cancer for the selected time period. Also, the darker color represents the higher death rate. From the map, we can see that southern states have highest death rate of lung cancer and the death rate flucates a lot across different states. It would be interesting to inverstgate what are the factors causing such flucation.
