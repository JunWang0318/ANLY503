Sub-Question 1: What is the overall trending for the the age adjusted cancer incidence rate, mortality rate and death rate?


In Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA), we propose five sub-questions.


Even though the incidence rate is increasing since 1999, it does not have meaningful indication because its computation does not exclude the age effects. For example, a county having a higher percentage of elderly people may have a higher rate of death or hospitalization than a county with a younger population, merely because the elderly are more likely to die or be hospitalized. In order to make fairer comparisons between groups with different age distributions, we introduce the variable called Age adjusting rate. It can make the different groups more comparable, and same as before, the number is represent the number of cancer records per 10,000 people. For future reference, for our study, all the rates we mention are age adjusted.
