Sub-Question 1: What is the overall trending for cancer incidence rate, mortality rate, and death rate?


In order to find out the overall trending for the cancer rates as well as the cancer counts, we decide to creat a 3D scatter plot with the x-axis be the year, y-axis represents the mortality count, and z-axis represents the incidence count.



Figure 4 (Plotly)


From the above 3D-scatter plot, the size and color of dots represent cancer incidence and mortality rate. The larger size and darker color dot have a large value of the cancer rate. For incidence and mortality rates, both with the unit of count per 100,000 people.The viewer can select the rate using the dropdown filter on the upper left corner. From the chart above, we can see that even though the total number of counts for both incidence and mortality are increasing since 1999, the actual rates are decreasing.


Next, we decide to take a closer look at the cancer incidence count and the cancer mortality count.


Figure 5 (Plotly)

From the above chart, we can see that the count for the cancer incidences is increasing since 1999, while the count for the cancer mortalities is more stabilized with slightly increasing through out the years. Therefore, to obtain a clear overall trending, we introduce a new variable feature - death rate which is computed by the number of mortalities divides the number of incidences for each year.


Figure 6 (Plotly)


From the line chart above, we can see that there was a significant drop for the death rate since 1999, then there was a fluctuation since 2009 and stabilized after 2013.
