Sub-Question 3: Is there a relationship between the cancer rate and the economic activities?


Since we are interested about the relationship between the cancer rate and the geographic location, we use tableau to create a map with the average cancer mortality rate from 2005 to 2015 for each states across united states.


Figure 16 (Tableau)


For the treemap above, darker colors represent higher average cancer mortality rate. As we can see, the top five states are Kentucky, Mississippi, West Virginia, Louisiana, and Arkansas. In addition, the least five states are Utah, Colorado, New Mexico, Arizona, and California. So now, we want to only focus on these 10 states and take a look at the economics activities for each states to investigate the relationship.


For each states, we plot the pie chart for its average GDP output from 2005 to 2015. In general, among the total 11 categories, the portions for Finance and Manufacturing vary the most compare to the rest of the categories.



Figure 17 (ggplot)


From the line chart above, we can see that there is a significant drop for the death rate since 1999, then there a fluctuations since 2009 and stabilized after 2013.
